Florida Statute § 817.505 — Patient Brokering Act

Felony-Level Criminal Exposure

The Florida Patient Brokering Act § 817.505 carries felony-level criminal exposure for fee-split arrangements with healthcare providers, applicable to ALL payers including self-pay. There is no carve-out for cash-pay or boutique optical.

Before launching ANY structured partnership program with neurologists, dentists, dermatologists, primary care, or other clinical providers, Eola must engage a Florida healthcare attorney for a $3,000-$8,000 written legal opinion covering each partnership archetype. Lifestyle, retail, and hospitality partnerships carry materially lower legal risk but still require disclosure of any financial relationship.

Partnership Models — Lawful Structures

Allowed structures

Lunch-and-Learn Brand education at clinical practices · no fee · OD-led
Co-Branded Trunk Show Joint event · revenue-neutral · each contributes inventory + space
Reciprocal Education Materials Patient-facing materials · no fee · mutual
Gift-with-Styling-Appointment Eola gives partner product on Eola purchase · no third-party fee
Cross-Promotional Content Mutual social mention, blog feature · no fee
Sponsorship + Advertising Standard sponsorship contract with cultural / community partners

NOT used — legal risk

Per-referral payment to any healthcare provider — violates Florida Patient Brokering Act § 817.505 + federal AKS / Stark.

Percentage-split arrangements on healthcare-referred patients — same statute.

Gift cards as referral incentive to clinical partners — treated as remuneration under the Patient Brokering Act.

Affiliate commissions on healthcare referrals — even with disclosure, prohibited.

Non-healthcare retail affiliate / commission arrangements are acceptable WITH disclosure.
