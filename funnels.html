Top 3 Internal Levers — The Headline

The combined lift from these three internal levers is $400-680K annual revenue (+12% to +21%) with zero new acquisition cost. Customers are already in the chair; the practice already paid for the demand. This is where the work belongs first.

Lever 1 18-36 months

Doctor-to-Optical Handoff + Tiered Pricing Menu

$200K-320K
Annual lift (capture rate 65% → 73-78%)
  • Warm intro from exam chair to optician with named recommendation
  • Good / Better / Best pricing menu: $350 / $650 / $1,200+
  • Capture-rate target move: 65% → 73-78% (top-quartile boutique)
  • Investment: $5K-10K (staff training, scripting, signage)

Why this is #1: Customers walking out with their Rx are the most expensive lost revenue in the practice. Eola paid for the exam, owns the demand, and watches the buy go elsewhere. Industry consensus: warm doctor handoff alone moves capture 5-15 pts.

Lever 2 18-24 months

Multi-Pair / Sunglass Attach Program

$80K-160K
Annual lift (attach 18% → 28%+)
  • In-optical signage at the fitting station
  • Second-pair offer presented during fitting (NOT at checkout)
  • Optician trained on lifestyle questions (sun exposure / computer use / hobbies)
  • Attach-rate target: 18% → 28% (best-in-class boutique 35-45%)

Why this is #2: Pure margin add. Patient is already in the chair. Frame is paid for. Sunglass / blue-light / computer-pair conversation is a 5-minute lift. Review of Optometric Business case study reaches 40% sunglass attach.

Lever 3 12-18 mo to full ramp

24-Month Recall + Insurance-Benefit-Expiry Program

$120K-200K
Annual lift (recall response 25% → 45%)
  • SMS at 18 / 22 / 24 months
  • Nov-Dec email push for unused vision benefits (use-it-or-lose-it)
  • Family-member offers
  • Recall response target: 25% → 45%

Why this is #3: Existing-customer revenue at zero CAC. The 24-mo window is a known industry replacement cycle. Failing to trigger it means handing the recall to a competitor.

Combined: $400-680K = +12% to +21% revenue

Zero new acquisition cost. All three levers are internal — they reshape what happens to the patients Eola already gets through the door.

External Acquisition Levers

De-prioritized. Fill the leaky bucket first. Marketing acquisition compounds the cost of every dropped capture-rate point. Run external levers in parallel only after internal economics have a measured baseline.

GBP Optimization + Reviews Cadence

  • Commit to 5-8 Google reviews per month (structured ask at pickup)
  • Professional photos refreshed quarterly
  • Weekly GBP posts (new arrivals, doctor spotlights)
  • Cheapest CAC channel after WOM ($30-92 capture-adjusted)

Premium Brand Merchandising + IG Lookbooks

  • Carry Cartier / Lindberg / Dita / Tom Ford visibly in store
  • IG content built around real Orlando neighborhoods + customers
  • Tests H-M3 (price sensitivity) + H-G1 (IG ROI) in-market
  • AOV mix-shift target: $650 → $720

Quarterly Trunk Shows

  • One brand per quarter (Cartier, Lindberg, Dita, Tom Ford)
  • Invite-only for top patients + warm prospects
  • Press / lifestyle creator coverage as content amplifier
  • Differentiates against Oxford Eyes' "private styling" program

Implementation Sequence

Internal levers FIRST (months 1-12). External acquisition SECOND (months 6-24).

Do not invest in marketing acquisition until the capture-rate baseline is measured from PMS data. Otherwise every paid CAC dollar gets multiplied by the leaky-bucket discount. Order matters.

  • Months 1-3: 30-day PMS data extract; baseline capture / AOV / attach / recall
  • Months 1-12: Lever 1 (handoff + tiered pricing) → Lever 2 (attach) → Lever 3 (recall)
  • Months 6-24: Layer external acquisition (GBP cadence, IG lookbooks, trunk shows)
  • Months 12-36: Re-measure capture rate; fund partnership lift only after legal foundation in place

Implications Engine

Distilled implications from funnel economics — what the data forces Eola to do, and what it forces Eola to stop doing.

What the data forces Eola TO DO

  • Pull a 30-day PMS extract before committing to any lift projection externally
  • Sequence internal levers FIRST — capture, attach, recall — over 18-36 months
  • Build a tiered Good / Better / Best pricing menu ($350 / $650 / $1,200+) and train opticians on it
  • Move the second-pair offer from checkout into the fitting conversation
  • Win at GBP / local SEO before paying for Google Ads or IG paid (3-7x cheaper CAC)
  • Carry visible premium designer inventory (Cartier / Lindberg / Dita / Tom Ford) — caps AOV near $500 without it
  • Use the structured pickup-time ask for Google reviews — single highest-ROI advocacy mechanic

What the data forces Eola to STOP DOING

  • Stop projecting 60-90 day timelines for capture-rate work — Red Team contradicts; reality is 18-36 months
  • Stop quoting Eola-specific lift numbers without PMS data — they are modeled, not measured
  • Stop using Instagram paid as direct response — it is brand and content; GBP is direct response
  • Stop bidding Google Ads for "eye exam Orlando" — commoditized, chains will outbid; use boutique-intent only
  • Stop chasing partnership-driven acquisition until Florida § 817.505 legal opinion is in hand
  • Stop participating in vision-plan directories that compress AOV — selective panel participation only
  • Stop treating opticians as fitters — they need to be stylists with lifestyle Q training

Bottom line: The single highest-leverage economic move at Eola is capture rate × AOV, not patient volume. A boutique should operate at 75-85% capture and $650-1,000 AOV — and the path there is internal lever discipline over 18-36 months, anchored to PMS-measured baselines, not early-draft 90-day projections.
