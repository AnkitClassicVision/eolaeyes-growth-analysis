Customer Personas

Six grounded archetypes for boutique optical sales at Eola Eyes — anchored to Census/Vision Council data and Orlando neighborhood demographics.

Hypothesis Validation Summary

H-M2 (35-65 affluent core, $100k+ HHI) strongly validated — Niche/Point2Homes data confirms Thornton Park, Baldwin Park, Winter Park, and Lake Nona households sit comfortably above $100k HHI.

H-M3 (price sensitivity LOW; style + trust > price) is bimodal — true for Cal, Jonathan, and Priya; qualified for Maya and Tess where insurance/benefits remain a meaningful subgroup.

H-G3 (awareness gap vs. Warby Parker / chains) holds for non-patient affluent professionals — the gap is specifically a top-of-funnel discovery deficit, not a blanket recognition problem.

Implications

SO WHAT

Maya is the largest segment by count; Cal is the highest LTV by ~5x.

  • Maya + Tess combined = largest addressable population in immediate trade area
  • Cal + Jonathan disproportionately drive revenue — Cal alone is ~5x Maya's 5-yr LTV
  • Revenue is not normally distributed — both flywheels matter (volume + value)

NOW WHAT

Three concurrent channel investments, ranked by ROI confidence.

  • Priority IG creative for Maya — current Warby user, IG-discoverable, content-producing
  • CRM + SMS email for Cal — "frame-of-the-month" drops, trunk shows, designer events
  • Clinical-referral pipeline for Priya & Michael — Neurolens, Avulux, peer-physician network

WHAT IF

Suburban migration may shift the gravitational center over the next 24–36 months.

  • If Warby opens in Mall at Millenia — tighten Maya's IG/local-creator content; convert faster to Cal trajectory
  • If Lake Nona doubles — Priya scales; consider satellite or pop-up presence
  • If consumer trade-down accelerates — Maya/Tess delay; Jonathan/Cal recession-resistant

COMPARED TO

Eola does not compete with Warby Parker on price, age, or online convenience.

  • Warby buyer: 18–35, mid-HHI (declining), $95–195 frames, standard lenses, house brand only
  • Eola buyer: 35–65, $150k+ HHI, $300–2,500 frames, Varilux X / Neurolens / Avulux, 6+ designer brands
  • Eola competes on the upgrade transition — the obvious next step after the Warby Parker years
