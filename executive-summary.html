Executive Summary

Boutique optical sales market opportunity, strategic priorities, and 24-month roadmap for Eola Eyes.

Market Opportunity Score

8.2 /10

Catchment with $100k+ verified household income, an Orlando MSA leading the nation in jobs, population, and GDP growth, and a luxury sub-segment that is structurally insulated from mass-market price compression. Score is held below 9 by Oxford Eyes' incumbency 1.7 miles north, the rentable nature of Eola's artisan brand portfolio, and capture-rate execution risk on the 18-36 month timeline industry case studies actually require.

Component Scoring

8.5 /10

Market Demand

Orlando MSA Triple Crown growth (2.7% population, 2.5% jobs, GDP leader); 5-ZIP catchment median household income approximately $103k; Baldwin Park 32814 at $140k median with 42.5% graduate-degree adults. DTC online share has reverted from 22% to 14%, reinforcing in-person boutique retail.

8.2 /10

Financial Returns

Base 5-year LTV $3,640 per boutique patient (10-year LTV $6,370). LTV:CAC ranges from 11:1 on Google Ads to 182:1 on word-of-mouth. Scenario B targets a $646K annual revenue lift; ~$400-680K of that is internal-lever, zero-CAC capture-rate and attach work.

7.0 /10

Competitive Position

Real fight is Oxford Eyes (Ivanhoe Row, 1.7 miles north, 7 consecutive Best of ORL wins). Brand-portfolio overlap on Lindberg, Tom Ford, and Silhouette means Eola's uniqueness gap is roughly 6 brands not 21. Eola's artisan portfolio (Blake Kuwahara, Matsuda, Theo, l.a.Eyeworks, ic!berlin, Robert Marc) is rentable, not owned. Warby Sport at $195 launched April 2026 adds partial direct threat.

7.5 /10

Implementation Risk

Florida Patient Brokering Act § 817.505 is felony-level for healthcare-referral arrangements and applies to all payers including self-pay. Capture-rate moves credibly take 18-36 months in published case studies, not 90 days. Internal-lever execution requires sustained doctor-handoff training and a PMS data extract before any lift projection is anchored.

Strategic Findings

Finding 1: Oxford Eyes is the real competitor.

1.7 miles north on Ivanhoe Row, 7 consecutive Best of ORL wins, IG audience roughly 62% larger than Eola, named private-styling program Eola lacks.

  • Brand overlap on Lindberg, Tom Ford, and Silhouette compresses the differentiation story
  • Uniqueness gap is realistically 6 artisan brands, not 21
  • Capacity already deployed at Ivanhoe Row with named experiential programming
Reframes H-C1 + H-C2

Finding 2: $400-680K zero-CAC lift exists, but 18-36 months, not 90 days.

Internal levers (capture rate +13 points, multi-pair attach +10 points, structured recall) deliver a 12-21% revenue lift without new acquisition spend.

  • Requires PMS data extract first to anchor lift projections to Eola's true baseline
  • Comparable benchmark literature (Review of Optometric Business) took 36 months
  • Needs OD doctor-to-optical handoff plus optician-as-stylist training investment
Anchors Scenario A and B

Finding 3: Maya = priority persona, with caveat.

Thornton Park Creative Director (35-45, $200k+ HHI), current Warby Parker user looking to upgrade. But creative-class national migration is partially reversing suburban — validate against Eola's actual customer ZIPs in the first 90 days.

  • Six personas total span professional, creative, clinical, and frame-wardrobe segments
  • Cal (Frame Wardrobe collector) carries the highest LTV at approximately $24,200
  • Dr. Priya (Lake Nona Med) anchors the clinical-pipeline partnership thesis
Refines H-M2 + H-G3

Finding 4: Partnership program needs a $3-8K legal opinion FIRST.

Florida Patient Brokering Act § 817.505 is felony-level for healthcare referrals. 80+ named Orlando prospects mapped, but no fee-flow with neurologists, dentists, or dermatologists without a bulletproof legal foundation.

  • Lifestyle, retail, and hospitality partnerships carry materially lower legal risk
  • Lunch-and-learn plus reciprocal-form model only for clinical partners
  • Statute applies to all payers including self-pay; no carve-out exists
Gates H-G2 launch

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

Anchor on data and legal foundation

  • PMS data extract: capture rate, AOV, attach rate, recall response (REQUIRED before lift projections)
  • Florida healthcare attorney engagement for Patient Brokering Act opinion ($3-8K)
  • GBP optimization plus Q1 review velocity push (target 5-8 reviews per month)
  • Email and SMS lifecycle on existing patient list
  • Stand up named private-styling program (close gap to Oxford)
Phase 2: Build (Months 4-12)

Differentiate on experience and brand

  • Quarterly trunk show calendar (Q2 launch with Lorelei Wine Bar; close Oxford trunk-show gap)
  • IG content engine: 4 posts per week plus 2 Reels per week, brand storytelling around artisan portfolio
  • Top-5 partnership outreach (post-legal-opinion): Neurovations, Lorelei Wine Bar, Park Smiles, Migraine Center, Grand Bohemian
  • Capture rate training rollout: doctor-to-optical handoff plus tiered pricing menu
  • Re-validate Maya persona against actual customer-base ZIP analysis
Phase 3: Scale (Months 13-24)

Compound capture rate and brand authority

  • Capture rate measurable lift (target +5-8 points by month 18, top-quartile by month 24)
  • Multi-pair attach program at scale
  • Press: Orlando Magazine, Bungalower, Edible Orlando feature pitches
  • Best of ORL voter mobilization for 2026 / 2027 (challenge Oxford's incumbency)
  • Influencer partnerships at the micro level (5k-50k Orlando lifestyle creators)
