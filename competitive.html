COMPARED TO

Eola's pricing band (~$800 avg ticket) is 3–8x Warby, 2–3x chain mid-range, and 25–40% below Beiner luxury. The middle squeeze (MyEyeDr, Aloma, mainstream-designer practice optical) is the most economically dangerous neighbor — they charge 50–60% of Eola's price for substitutable Tom Ford / YSL / Gucci / Maui Jim frames. Differentiation cannot rest on price; it must rest on clinical authority (3 ODs since 2003), curated indie portfolio (Blake Kuwahara, Matsuda, Theo), and an experience layer Eola has not yet built.
