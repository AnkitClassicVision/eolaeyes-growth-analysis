Marketing Strategy

Boutique-as-curated-experience positioning + 12-month channel mix for Eola Eyes.

H-G1 Reframed (0.68 → 0.50): GBP and Local SEO beat paid Instagram on CAC

Phase 3 review downgraded the "Instagram is the highest-ROI channel" hypothesis. In the optometry data, Google Business Profile and local SEO deliver CAC of $30-92 per booked patient versus $230-460 for paid Instagram. The right read for Eola: Instagram is the BRAND layer (discovery, aesthetic, trust), GBP is the FUNNEL layer (high-intent capture). Both need investment. Neither replaces the other.

Funnel layer
$30-92 CAC

GBP + local SEO. Captures "near me" intent the moment someone is searching for an Orlando optometrist.

Brand layer
$230-460 CAC

Paid Instagram. Builds aesthetic memory and the curated-boutique perception. Does not replace funnel capture.

Headwind
AI Overviews

Google AI Overviews are eroding GBP click-through. Defend with reviews, posts, photos, and parallel IG content gravity.

Neurolens for misalignment headache, Avulux for photophobia, Stellest for pediatric myopia, Meta AI Glasses, Nuance Audio.

Pillar 4

Orlando-grown since 2003.

Mills 50, Thornton Park, Lake Eola — local before it was cool. 23 years on Mills Avenue.

Pillar 5

Boutique without pretension.

Independent, women-owned, patient-focused. The opposite of a chain.

Discipline rule

Every IG post, email, and press touch carries one of these five pillars. If a piece of content doesn't, kill it. Pillar 3 (tech-forward lenses) is the most under-leveraged Eola asset.

Channel Mix & Budget Allocation

Eye-care industry standard for marketing budget is 1-5% of gross revenue. Boutique cash-pay practices anchor 3-5%. Three tiers below for a 3-OD single-location boutique.

Tier Monthly Annual Channels included
Lite $4,000 $48,000 GBP optimization + reviews push, organic IG, 1 micro-influencer/quarter, 1 trunk show/yr
Standard $7,500 $90,000 + paid IG/FB, email/SMS lifecycle, 4 micro-influencer collabs/yr, 2 trunk shows/yr, press pitches
Aggressive $12,000 $144,000 + Google Ads, sponsored Orlando publication content, 4 trunk shows/yr, paid creator program, podcast sponsorship

Recommendation: Standard tier ($7,500/mo). Right altitude for a 3-OD boutique with Eola's catalog depth. Phase: Q1 fix GBP + email/SMS, Q2 layer IG paid + creator, Q3-Q4 layer press + Google Ads.

Implications Engine

Risk — Misallocated channel mix

Over-investing in IG paid (CAC $230-460) without first stacking GBP equity (CAC $30-92) leaves cash ROI on the table. IG is the brand layer; GBP is the funnel layer. Both, not either.

Action — Sequence the 12-month plan

Q1: Fix GBP, push reviews, build email/SMS lifecycle on the existing patient list. Q2: Layer paid IG, creator collabs, trunk show. Q3-Q4: Layer Google Ads, press, sponsored content. One named owner per workstream.

Risk — Discipline + persona drift

"Boutique" without daily proof becomes a dead positioning word. Posting fragments without messaging discipline dilutes brand. 786-follower IG is a multi-quarter growth project, not a quick win.

Decision — Standard tier ($7,500/mo)

Right altitude for a 3-OD boutique with Eola's catalog depth. Build the GBP owner role, the email/SMS lifecycle, and the quarterly trunk show calendar. Win Best of ORL 2026. Pillar 3 (tech-forward lenses) is the most under-leveraged Eola asset to scale.
